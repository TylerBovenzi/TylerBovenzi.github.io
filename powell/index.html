Trip Overview

We're planning a 7-day houseboat expedition on Lake Powell - one of the most spectacular water recreation destinations in the country. With 1,900+ miles of shoreline and 96 side canyons, Lake Powell offers unlimited opportunities for water sports, exploration, and relaxation.

Why June 19-26 Works Best

Juneteenth Weekend: Federal holiday gives us a long weekend to start
Perfect Weather: 92°F air temperature, 70°F water - ideal for all activities
Fewer Crowds: Pre-peak season means better access to prime locations
Cost Savings: 20-30% less expensive than July peak season

Current Lake Conditions: Lake Powell is at 47% capacity, which means some shallow areas may be inaccessible, but the main lake and most popular destinations remain fully navigable.

What Makes June Better Than July

  • Water temperature perfect for swimming (70°F+) without needing wetsuits
  • Long days with 13+ hours of sunlight and minimal rainfall
  • Significantly fewer people compared to July peak season
  • Better availability for equipment rentals and prime camping spots
  • Major cost savings on all rentals and accommodations
  • Still excellent fishing conditions from spring season

Fish & Wildlife at Lake Powell

Lake Powell's diverse ecosystem supports abundant fish populations and fascinating desert wildlife. Understanding what you might encounter enhances both fishing success and wildlife appreciation during your expedition.

Prime Fishing Species

Lake Powell offers world-class fishing opportunities with species ranging from trophy bass to massive catfish. June conditions are excellent for most species.

June Fishing Conditions

Water Temperature: 70-75°F - ideal for active feeding
Best Times: Early morning (5-8 AM) and evening (6-9 PM)
Technique: Focus on structure like submerged rocks and canyon walls

Game Fish

Largemouth Bass

Average: 1-3 lbs | Trophy: 5+ lbs
Most popular sport fish in Lake Powell. Found around rocky structure, points, and submerged vegetation. Very active in June with excellent topwater action during morning and evening hours.
Game Fish

Smallmouth Bass

Average: 1-2 lbs | Trophy: 4+ lbs
Aggressive fighters found in deeper rocky areas and canyon walls. Peak activity in June as water warms. Known for spectacular jumping when hooked.
Game Fish

Striped Bass

Average: 3-8 lbs | Trophy: 15+ lbs
Open water predators that school and feed aggressively. Look for bird activity on the surface indicating feeding schools. Can provide fast action when located.
Trophy Fish

Channel Catfish

Average: 2-5 lbs | Trophy: 15+ lbs
Bottom feeders active at night. Excellent eating fish. Use cut bait or stink baits in deeper areas and creek channels. Some reach massive sizes in Lake Powell.
Sport Fish

Walleye

Average: 1-3 lbs | Trophy: 5+ lbs
Excellent table fare found in deeper waters. Most active during low light conditions. Use jigs or live bait near bottom structure in 20-40 feet of water.
Panfish

Bluegill

Average: 6-8 inches | Large: 10+ inches
Perfect for beginners and steady action. Found in shallow coves and protected areas. Use small baits and light tackle for best success.
Invasive

Common Carp

Average: 5-15 lbs | Large: 20+ lbs
Large, powerful fish found throughout the lake. While not native, they provide strong fights and are sometimes targeted by specialized anglers.
Rare

Northern Pike

Average: 3-8 lbs | Trophy: 15+ lbs
Toothy predators found in certain areas of the lake. Excellent fighters but require careful handling. Use wire leaders and focus on weed lines and structure.

Desert Wildlife & Birds

The surrounding desert ecosystem and lake environment support diverse wildlife that you may encounter during hikes and shoreline exploration.

Birds

Great Blue Heron

Large wading birds commonly seen fishing in shallow areas. Excellent indicators of good fishing spots. Often spotted standing motionless near shore.
Birds

Double-crested Cormorant

Diving birds that pursue fish underwater. When you see them actively diving, there are likely schools of fish in the area - great fishing intelligence.
Birds

Osprey

Fish-eating raptors with spectacular diving abilities. Nesting platforms installed around the lake support healthy populations. Amazing to watch fish.
Mammals

Desert Bighorn Sheep

Occasionally seen on steep canyon walls and rocky slopes. Rare sightings but possible during quiet morning or evening hours near water sources.
Mammals

Mule Deer

Common in canyon areas and come to water during dawn and dusk. May be seen along shorelines or hiking trails, especially near freshwater seeps.
Reptiles

Lizards (Various Species)

Numerous lizard species including collared, fence, and whiptail lizards. Commonly seen sunning on rocks. Harmless and interesting to observe.

Wildlife Safety & Considerations

Rattlesnakes: Present in desert areas. Wear proper footwear when hiking and watch where you step.
Scorpions: Check shoes and clothing before putting them on. Shake out sleeping bags.
Food Storage: Secure all food to prevent wildlife encounters, especially ravens and ringtails.
Respectful Distance: Observe all wildlife from a distance and never attempt to feed animals.

Fishing Equipment Recommendations

Bass Fishing: Medium-action spinning rods, 10-15 lb line, topwater lures, plastic worms
Striper Fishing: Heavy action rods, 20+ lb line, large spoons, live bait rigs
Catfish: Heavy rods, 20+ lb line, circle hooks, cut bait, stink baits
General: Net, tackle box, pliers, fillet knife, cooler with ice
